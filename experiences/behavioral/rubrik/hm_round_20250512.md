## Part 2: Behavioral Questions (~15 min)

### Q: Did you ever mentor a new joiner? How?
**My answer:**
- Yes, mentored a new joiner
- Improved the new joiner ramp-up documentation
- Assigned them bugs and had regular syncs to understand progress
- Even when I knew the fix, I didn't share it — let them debug independently to build self-sufficiency
- Outcome: The new joinee was very fast, had great activity and initiative
- She later moved to another team due to a reorg

### Q: How do you mentor your teammates?
**My answer:**
- Classified teammates into two groups: **leaders** and **followers**
- **Leaders:** I interact, discuss ideas, brainstorm together. I give them liberty to pick items or help me on my items. Collaborative, peer-level dynamic.
- **Followers:** I encourage them to think independently and own an area. My scope of mentoring is more limited — guide them toward ownership.

### Q: Why did the project take 1.5 years?
**My answer:**
- AI capabilities (LLM tooling, orchestration platforms) only matured towards end of 2025
- Before that, the platform wasn't ready for production-grade autonomous agents
- The project evolved through phases as the technology caught up

### Q: How are you using AI? How is AI benefiting your work?
**My answer:**
- Metric: In the last month, pushed 30K+ lines of code — way more velocity than pre-AI
- He pushed back: "Lines of code is fine, but what's the actual productivity gain?"
- Better answer: 
  - Multiple AI agents maintained by developers — when doing multiple POCs, I delegate to different agents and parallelize my doc writing or other development work simultaneously
  - Feature planning: use AI to verify design docs, cross-check assumptions
  - Net effect: can run multiple workstreams in parallel that would have been sequential before

## Retrospective

### What likely went wrong (speculative)

0. **~~Experience-team mismatch~~ — RULED OUT**
   - Passed 5 multithreading coding rounds + 2 system design rounds before HM
   - Rubrik had the resume before HM round — no surprises about background
   - Technical bar was already cleared. The rejection came from the HM round itself.
   - HM rounds evaluate leadership signal, culture fit, communication — not technical domain match

1. **Project discussion redirect at ~60% through**
   - Spent ~20 min with diagrams and detailed optimizations. HM said "that's fine, you can explain more, but what are the challenges?"
   - This is actually normal HM behavior — they steer the conversation toward what they want to evaluate
   - 20 min of detailed project discussion in a 50 min interview is reasonable pacing, not a red flag
   - The redirect itself is unlikely the reason for rejection

2. **"Leaders vs followers" framing**
   - This is a culture red flag for many companies, especially one with "Integrity" and "Excellence" as core values
   - Implies you've categorized teammates into tiers, which reads as dismissive
   - **Lesson**: Reframe as "I adapt my mentoring style — collaborative brainstorming with experienced engineers, more structured guidance with newer ones"

3. **Lines of code as AI productivity metric**
   - Got explicit pushback. If the HM had to correct you mid-answer, that's a negative signal — it shows you hadn't thought about the real value proposition
   - **Lesson**: Lead with outcomes (parallel workstreams, faster iteration cycles, reduced time-to-POC), not volume

4. **1.5 year justification**
   - "AI wasn't ready" can sound passive — like you were waiting rather than driving
   - **Lesson**: Frame as phased delivery with independent value at each stage

### What went well
- Challenges answer was specific and technical (scaling, hallucination, correlation ID)
- Mentoring new joiner answer showed good instincts (didn't spoon-feed fixes)

### Honest assessment
- The project discussion itself was likely fine — 20 min with diagrams and depth is solid
- The rejection more likely came from behavioral signals: terminology ("leaders/followers"), metrics (lines of code), or something subtle in how answers landed
- Without detailed feedback, it's hard to pinpoint — but the behavioral section is the most likely culprit
- HM rounds weigh culture fit and leadership signal heavily — technical depth alone doesn't carry them
