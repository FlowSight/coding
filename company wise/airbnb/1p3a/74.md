I received a call from the recruiter, and we briefly discussed the experience on my resume. At the end, they asked if I had Kubernetes experience. I described our project built on Kubernetes and my operating experience. Finally, the recruiter said he wasn't very familiar with distributed systems and felt they should be hiring someone to write Kubernetes code rather than someone on top of Kubernetes, saying I wasn't a good fit. He said he'd need to talk to the team and would give me about two days. After that, I looked at the job description again, and it seemed like a perfect match for my background. Who wants to write Kubernetes code for no reason?

Sure enough, they rejected me the next day. It felt really strange. 😰


Even if you pass the initial screening (VO) at Airbnb, it's the same at TM (Team Tech). It's not a 100% match. You might be able to design and lead projects without any ramp-up and still get rejected (like me). It's not your fault; Infraorg's requirements for background matching are a bit too much and short-sighted. For the sake of short-term gains and a couple of months of ramp-up time, you're missing out on better candidates.
