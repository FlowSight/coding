I got an Airbnb interview opportunity through a referral and applied for Airbnb G10.
The interview process is similar to other events.
The phone interview focused on algorithms .
The virtual onsite interview had four rounds: experience deepdive, system design , algorithms, front-end (because I applied for full-stack) , and then team matching. Only one team was hiring. There was also a core value round, which was essentially behavior, but you had to demonstrate Airbnb's core values, such as hospitality, entrepreneurial spirit, etc. The interview was relatively simple. The algorithm question involved a tree, where the leaves are numbers and the parent node represents the sum of its children. The task was to obtain the value of any node. The second step was to provide a mutation that could turn a node into a leaf or add a subtree to a leaf to become a parent. Again, you had to be able to find the value of any node. The front-end question involved writing a retry function, a higher-order function. This involved wrapping a function that might throw an error and providing different retry modes: retry once, retry n times, and exponential back-off retry. Design a watchlist system for renting an Airbnb listing. Users can post their desired stay dates on an Airbnb listing, and receive notifications if someone cancels during that time period. Hosts can also see how many watchlists are on their listings.
