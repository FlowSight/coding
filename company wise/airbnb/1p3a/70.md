The final round of SD (Surveyor of Private Servers) was quite interesting. It was an L7 interviewer on a flight booking problem. I think the key concepts were graph search, considering the possibility of connecting flights and the trade-off between consistency and availability, as prices fluctuate. This guy explained the problem, clarified the requirements, and then started working on it. When I asked him questions, he just mumbled "uh-huh." After working for a while, I pointed out that my solution was fundamentally wrong, even though it was based on information he had previously confirmed. He seemed bothered that I hadn't asked him about the PA vs. PC trade-off before proceeding with my solution. He questioned me briefly before returning to my work. I was exhausted from being questioned and didn't want to talk anymore. There was virtually no communication throughout. This interviewer's perspective felt too subjective, even based on his own logical framework, and there was no communication. There was no dialogue whatsoever. Next time, I hope I won't interview with this kind of interviewer again; I'd rather not interview at all.
