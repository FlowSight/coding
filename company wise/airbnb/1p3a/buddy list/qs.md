followup problem gives a maximum value (max). It finds the maximum number of cities in your buddy's wishlist that are not in your wishlist, and sorts them based on the degree of overlap between your buddy and your

wishlist. For example, if your wishlist is a, b, c, d,
and buddy1's wishlist is a, b, e, f, two of which are the same as yours, then buddy1 is your buddy. If
buddy2's wishlist is a, c, d, g, three of which are the same as yours, then buddy2 is also your buddy.

The problem is to output a list of recommended cities with a maximum size of max.
When size is 10, cities in buddy1 and buddy2's wishlists that are not in your wishlist can be added to the recommendations. Because buddy2 has a higher overlap, cities in buddy2's wishlist are output first, so the recommendations are g, e, f.
When size is 2, the recommendations are g, e or g, f.
