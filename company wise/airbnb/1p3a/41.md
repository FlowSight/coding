Sharing my experience with the full-stack phone interview for Airbed, where I applied for a position at San Francisco Hybrid.

The process was quick; I received a phone interview invitation from HR a week after submitting my resume.

It was a 60-minute round: the first 40 minutes were live coding, and the last 20 minutes were a product discussion.

The topic was creating a small component for booking accommodations.
Enter your check-in and check-out dates to calculate the total price.
Select the number of people (adults/children), and the price varies depending on the number of people.
If the room is full, the button will turn gray.
Click "Book Now" to bring up a confirmation dialog box.
I implemented it using React + Node.js, with Zustand managing the frontend state and a simple API mock written in Express for the backend.
The follow-up question was:
How should the front-end and back-end be designed to support multi-currency display?
How to reduce the number of requests from the frontend? (Answer: Batch API calls/GraphQL)
How can we cache booking information offline? (Answer: IndexedDB + Service Worker)
Overall, I answered quite well, but I didn't consider security in the offline caching section. The interviewer reminded me to handle sensitive information encryption.
Two days later, I received an onsite invitation.
My overall impression: Airbed prioritizes user experience and product scalability . Code that runs is just the foundation; more importantly, it considers internationalization, multi-platform adaptation, and user experience optimization.
