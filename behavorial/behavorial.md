## Staff Engineer Interview — What's Really Evaluated

A Staff Engineer is not evaluated only on whether they know distributed systems, databases, queues, caches, or algorithms. That is expected. The real signal is different:
- Can you take an ambiguous problem and make it clear?
- Can you explain trade-offs without sounding theoretical?
- Can you push back without sounding difficult?
- Can you influence people who do not report to you?
- Can you simplify a system instead of making it look impressive?
- Can you make decisions that are good for the customer, the business, and the engineering team?

This is where many strong engineers underprepare. They prepare for coding.
They prepare for system design. But they do not prepare enough for judgment.

### Stories to Prepare
- A time I created clarity in ambiguity.
- A time I disagreed with a senior stakeholder.
- A time I changed my mind after feedback.
- A time I improved engineering quality beyond my own code.
- A time I made a trade-off between speed and long-term quality.
- A time I helped another engineer or team succeed.
- A time I prevented future problems by improving systems, not just fixing symptoms.
